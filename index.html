What I Do

I am a generalist in the computing field. I can help with PC's, Macs, and mobile devices. I can do everything from helping you set up your email to programming a custom business application. I do hardware maintenance and upgrades on desktop PC’s and laptops, but not on mobile devices. I can also do custom built computers for gaming or other uses. I mainly serve the central Vermont area and am focused around Morrisville and Stowe. I do not carry an inventory. Any parts that must be replaced or upgraded, I will work with you to purchase. I also do not have a shop. While I do work on hardware and development out of my home, my other work is done at the customer’s place of residence, business or at a location where we can meet. Some things you may want to have me look at are:

  • Malware recovery and repair: Clean up the mess after a nasty virus or worm.
  • System Backup Setup: Keep your data from being lost in the event of a disaster. Backup your data.
  • Operating system updates and repair: Make sure your operating system is working smoothly.
  • Web filter Solutions: Keep your children safe from inappropriate content on the web.
  • Antivirus Solutions: Keep your computer secure from malware and hackers.
  • Tune-up software solutions: Optimize your PC with the best tune-up software available.
  • Hardware Upgrades and Repair: PC slow? A RAM or Hard drive upgrade may be what you need.
  • Software troubleshooting: Need help getting software up and running?
  • Internet troubleshooting: Internet Slow? The ISP can make mistakes.
  • Network setup
  • Home entertainment network & device setup

How much It Is

I’m currently charging $30.00 per hour but I offer a sliding scale fee. If you are on a limited budget, I can work with you. I also offer free first consultations. If you are outside of the Morristown area, there may be a travel fee. This small fee helps me keep up my car and pay for gas.

My Experience

My experience comes from several directions. In the US Marines, I was trained on Microsoft and Cisco systems and worked on DOD networks. I was responsible for everything from PC repair to configuring network routers and switches. I was also certified on Microsoft systems at this point. On leaving the Marines, I worked as a network consultant and a network administrator with local VT companies. There I learned a wide range of systems including Sonicwall, Citrix, Linux, HP and more. I then decided to use my GI bill and, with the help of the VA, started going to college for computer science. There, I received a lot of training in computer programming and web programming. In total, I have been in computing for around 13 years.
